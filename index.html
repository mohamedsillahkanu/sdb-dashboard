Overview
School-Based Distribution is a strategy aimed at maintaining universal coverage of Insecticidal Treated Nets ITNs) through schools. Faith- and community-based networks, agricultural and food-security support schemes can be explored as channels for ITN distribution in countries where such approaches are feasible and equitable (WHO and Global Malaria Programme, 2017) The Alliance for Malaria Prevention (AMP) have also encouraged the inclusion of other distribution channels like schools and community-based ITNs distribution as strategies to achieve and sustain universal coverage.  ITNs have contributed to significant reductions in malaria morbidity and mortality in recent years. Sustaining high coverage and use of insecticide-treated nets (ITN) is now a goal of most national malaria control and elimination programmes.
Key Objectives
• Achieve at least 95% registration of primary school pupils in classes 1, 3, and 5 in target districts
• Distribute ITNs to at least 95% of registered pupils in classes 1, 3, and 5 by the end of 2025
• Educate at least 95% of these pupils on proper ITN use and care for malaria prevention
• Ensure at least 80% of targeted pupils sleep under an ITN by the end of 2025
Expected Outcomes
• Identify the optimal ITN distribution infrastructure between MoH and MBSSE in Sierra Leone
• Document lessons learnt to inform future rollouts of the ITN School-Based Distribution (SBD) strategy
• Validate SBD as an effective strategy for achieving universal ITN coverage in Sierra Leone
• Ensure at least 95% of primary school pupils in classes 1, 3, and 5 receive an ITN by the end of 2025
• Ensure at least 95% of targeted pupils have knowledge of proper ITN use and care by the end of 2025

Conclusion & Recommendations
The School-Based Distribution of ITNs has demonstrated significant success in achieving comprehensive coverage across Bo and Bombali districts. The strategic use of educational infrastructure has proven effective in reaching both rural and urban communities.
